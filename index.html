Television Marketing Case Study

To what extent does a television company's identity brand, especially those with a long history, psychologically influence a community's interest or nostalgia?

What It's About

This project that I have been working on over the course of three months consists of the exploration of how television marketing and identity come back to a community or spark nostalgia into it.

This project informs audiences about those strategies and how television stations use it. The primary focus of this study included topics like graphic design and strategic marketing techniques. Not to mention, how the preservation of a marketing campaign comes down to the psychological needs and demands from an audience.

What Inspired Me

This project was inspired by my love of logo-making and graphic design, which had begun since I was younger and still to this day. I primarily had an interest on graphic design for television than other forms of it. I believed it had an impact not just on me, but also to an audience. My interests into marketing also carried onto my interest and helped me to draw the connections of graphic design and clients. Aside from visuals that define a television network, There was a community event held at Glenbrook South High School, by CBS News Chicago. It was called the "School Spotlight", which showcases high schools around the Chicago area and how they have an impact on their students, as well as highlight features of the high schools which make them stand out compared to others. Through my participation in this program, it helped me bulid more information on how television connects people for generations to come aside from logos that appear during ad breaks, or the content they put out to the public.
